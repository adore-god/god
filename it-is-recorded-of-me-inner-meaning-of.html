"It Is Recorded of Me": The Inner Meaning of Psalm 40:7–8

There comes a moment when you realise the Bible is not speaking of external figures—it is speaking of you.

Then I said, See, I come; it is recorded of me in the roll of the book. My delight is to do your pleasure, O my God; truly, your law is in my heart. — Psalm 40:7–8

This is the voice of your assumed identity (Ehyeh/I AM) asserting itself within present consciousness (YHVH/LORD). It is not external; it is the inner declaration of I AM—the self recognising itself as the one through whom reality will be enacted. The “roll of the book” represents the statutes of consciousness that Elohim enforces, the judicial record of your assumed identity being upheld in reality. Here, the triad is active: YHVH/LORD presents the desire to align, Ehyeh/I AM declares readiness and union, and Elohim enforces the outcome within the mind.

“My delight is to do your pleasure, O my God; truly, your law is in my heart.” — Psalm 40:8

To “do God’s pleasure” is the present consciousness (YHVH/LORD) yielding to the I AM (Ehyeh/I AM). Delight signifies the experiential resonance of alignment. The “law in my heart” is Elohim operating in the subconscious, enforcing the statutes of creation. This demonstrates THREAD 2: Judgement and alignment—present consciousness recognises desire, the assumed identity claims sufficiency, and Elohim ensures its manifestation. There is no external law; the law is the structured enforcement of assumed identity in consciousness.

You Are the One It’s Written About

The triad here is fully realised: your present consciousness (YHVH/LORD) recognises the assumed I AM, and Elohim enforces that this identity dominates experience. Neville emphasised that the Bible is a psychological drama, not history. Each figure and event symbolises consciousness mechanics: YHVH/LORD presenting the current state, Ehyeh/I AM assumed within imagination, and Elohim enforcing the reality of that state. This Psalm is a declaration of your awakening; you are the one it’s written about. You are the Word made flesh within your own awareness. As you consciously occupy this I AM, Elohim ensures that the external and internal reality align with your assumed identity.

In Neville Goddard’s teachings, the passage reflects a pivotal realisation: the Bible is the story of consciousness unfolding within you. Each event, each person, each law is a manifestation of the triad. The reader, through present consciousness recognising the I AM and allowing Elohim to enforce it, becomes the protagonist of all scripture. The creative power is inherent; the triad mechanism ensures that your inner declarations are realised externally. You are the creator, and the entire Bible is your story enacted through the triad of awareness, assumed identity, and enforcement.

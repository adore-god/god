About This Site

Explore Neville Goddard’s teachings in a structured and accessible way, with direct references to the Bible, which itself reveals the Law of Assumption through its stories and symbolism.

When Neville spoke in his lectures, he always taught alongside the Bible because he was revealing what it had already shown—its psychological principles and processes. Today, many fail to recognise that the Bible is not just a reference but the very revelation Neville was making accessible.

About This Approach

The Bible is often approached as a historical document or a guide to external behaviour, interpreted within frameworks of theology that assume God exists outside of us. This perspective emphasises morality, ritual, and rules, which can make the text feel distant, prescriptive, or inaccessible. Many readers sense something deeper in the Bible but struggle to reconcile it with traditional doctrine.

Over decades of studying consciousness-based teachings, manifestation, and symbolic systems — from Tarot and Astrology to Alchemy, Wicca, and the work of modern teachers like Eckhart Tolle and Esther Hicks — a pattern became clear: much of this symbolism traces back to the Bible itself. That realisation reshaped the approach to Scripture entirely.

Neville Goddard’s work provides a foundation for understanding the Bible as a psychological text. He revealed that it describes the operations of the mind rather than historical events. However, his writings often retained conventional language about “God” and “man,” which can make it difficult for readers to fully internalise the concept of awareness as the creative power.

This site takes that insight further by engaging directly with the Bible itself, free from doctrinal assumptions. Using Neville’s framework as a guide, each passage is examined for consistent symbolism and internal logic. The result reveals a text that is cohesive, repetitive by design, and focused entirely on inner states rather than external circumstances.

The characters and narratives are symbolic: they describe how identity is assumed, how belief is impressed, and how the mind responds to what is imagined and accepted as true. Observing these patterns in the text allows the reader to see the same mechanisms at work within their own consciousness.

Engaging with the Bible in this way requires honesty and self-reflection. It may uncover ingrained assumptions and emotional habits, but it also offers clarity and freedom. Insight comes not from worshipping an external God, but from recognising awareness itself as the creative capacity to assume, become, and act from imagined states.

The Way exists to make that clarity accessible. This site documents a structured exploration of Neville Goddard’s work alongside careful, line-by-line study of Scripture as a practical record of imagination in operation — a guide to conscious assumption and living from “I AM that I AM.”

The striking realisation is that a book written thousands of years ago contains precise insights into the mechanics of the human mind. Its symbolism is functional rather than abstract, revealing how perception forms, identity stabilises, and change occurs. For anyone who has sensed the truth of the Bible but struggled with its traditional presentation, this approach offers clarity, practicality, and a path to internal understanding.

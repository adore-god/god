Genesis 1: CREATION

"For what can be known about God is plain to them, because God has shown it to them.
For his invisible attributes, namely, his eternal power and divine nature, have been clearly perceived, ever since the creation of the world, in the things that have been made." — Romans 1:19-20

The first chapter of Genesis is not a literal account of external events but a symbolic journey through the unfolding of consciousness. The Garden represents the blossoming mind, and each “day” depicts the emergence and embodiment of inner states from formless awareness to full living reality. Water, earth, light, and seed are gentle symbols of how latent potentials assume form and are sustained.

The Symbolic Power of Seven

The number seven appears again and again in Scripture, quietly marking cycles of completion, purification, and inner fulfilment. In the creation narrative, seven days trace the full movement of imagined states from awakening to rest, showing the rhythm by which consciousness unfolds and stabilises.

This pattern continues later in the Bible. Jesus speaks of forgiving “seventy times seven,” signalling not merely repetition but the limitless capacity of assumed states to be renewed and restored. In Genesis, Lamech invokes seventy times seven, echoing the same principle: cycles of consequence and restoration operate within a defined yet expansive rhythm. Even in symbolic accounts such as Mary Magdalene, the seven demons cast out represent the clearing of limiting states, making space for a fully assumed identity to emerge.

Throughout the narrative, seven signals wholeness. It marks the completion of a cycle and the readiness for transformation. Each instance—whether a day of creation, an act of forgiveness, or the casting out of demons—demonstrates that inner states follow a rhythm of assumption, purification, and fulfilment. In this way, seven threads through Scripture as a quiet guide to the natural movement of consciousness toward alignment with its chosen reality.

In The Beginning

When Genesis declares, “Let us make man in our image,” it is describing the moment when inner forces coordinate to make an imagined state visible. The mind organizes itself to reflect what has been assumed, and every inner law is faithfully expressed in the outer experience.

Genesis 1:1-2 – In the beginning, God created the heavens and the earth. The earth was without form and void, and darkness was over the face of the deep. And the Spirit of God was hovering over the face of the waters

Day One: Let There Be Light

Symbol: The first awakening of awareness

At first, the mind is like deep, unbroken water—dark, undefined, and waiting. Then comes the first glimmer: a quiet recognition of being. This light is not yet formed into specific expression; it is simply the initial sense of existence. In this moment, the mind separates awareness from unawareness, creating the possibility for directed imagination.

Day Two: The Firmament

Symbol: The emergence of inner order

The firmament divides waters above from waters below, representing the recognition that what is imagined internally is distinct from what appears externally. This separation allows higher states to influence the unfolding of outer circumstances while maintaining a structured balance between inner cause and visible effect.

Day Three: Dry Land and Seed Within Itself

Symbol: Stable ground and self-contained potential

Dry land rises from the waters, providing a foundation for new forms. Each seed carries the full pattern of what it will become. In this way, imagined states are contained within themselves, ready to manifest. Just as an acorn contains the oak it will become, every assumption planted in the mind already holds the fullness of its future expression.

Day Four: Sun and Moon

Symbol: Guiding light and reflective imagination

Two great lights appear: one directs, the other receives and reflects. The sun represents active awareness guiding attention and focus, while the moon reflects that light, shaping and sustaining the images in consciousness. Together, they ensure balance between direct action and receptive formation, allowing inner assumptions to align with visible experience.

Day Five: Life in Water and Air

Symbol: Emotional animation and expansion

Fish swim in the seas, and birds fly in the sky. These lively forms depict assumptions coming alive, moving, multiplying, and filling the inner world with emotion and energy. Each feeling strengthens the state that has been assumed, spreading it throughout consciousness like ripples across water or currents in the air.

Day Six: Creatures and Man in God’s Image

Symbol: Full embodiment of chosen states

Living creatures appear on land, culminating in man, who embodies the assumed state completely. The imagined reality is no longer a possibility or wish; it is lived. This is the moment when consciousness steps fully into its creative role, directing and sustaining all inner patterns as a living, experienced identity.

Day Seven: Rest — The Sabbath

Symbol: Assurance and inner stillness

Completion brings rest. This is not physical rest, but the serene certainty that every assumption has taken root and will continue to unfold naturally. There is no striving, no questioning—only inner harmony and the confident knowledge that what is imagined will manifest according to its nature.

The Seven Movements of the First Assumption

Through these seven symbolic "days," Genesis traces the inner journey of every imagined state:

  1. Awakening to awareness of being.
  2. Separating inner cause from outer reflection.
  3. Establishing stable ground for potential to grow.
  4. Balancing directing and receptive forces within imagination.
  5. Animating the state through feeling and motion.
  6. Fully embodying the state as lived reality.
  7. Resting in certainty that the work is complete.

Every desire begins this silent cycle again. Each “Let there be light” is a soft dawn within consciousness, carrying the promise of a new inner world. As Neville said, “Creation is finished. It is your acceptance of it that makes it real to you.”

Proverbs 28:2 – When a land transgresses, it has many rulers, but with a man of understanding and knowledge, its stability will long continue.

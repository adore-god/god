Abraham: Faith

Neville Goddard taught that the Bible is a psychological drama unfolding within the life of each individual. For Neville, the Promise is a literal assurance that what you assume as your I AM will manifest. The stories in Scripture, especially those of Abraham, reveal this law: assumption creates reality.

In Genesis 15, the covenant with Abram is not only a divine contract; it is a blueprint for the inner workings of consciousness. Every symbol—from the stars to the sacrifice—represents the triad in action: YHVH/LORD as present consciousness, Ehyeh/I AM as assumed identity, and Elohim as the enforcing mechanism. Through this lens, Genesis 15 maps the operation of the Promise within the self.

God’s Assurance: The Shield and Reward

After these things, the word of the Lord came to Abram in a vision, saying, Have no fear, Abram: I will keep you safe, and great will be your reward

Symbolism: The shield is present consciousness protecting the assumed identity from doubt; the reward is Elohim enforcing the fulfilment of what has been assumed. Imagination both defends and actualises the Promise.

Abram’s Doubt: Childlessness

Sovereign Lord, what can you give me since I remain childless…?

Symbolism: The question embodies YHVH/LORD’s resistance to the unseen. Doubt arises naturally when a new I AM is assumed, yet it frames the space in which Elohim will execute the Promise.

The Stars and the Promise

Look up at the sky and count the stars—if indeed you can count them. So shall your offspring be.

Symbolism: The stars are infinite potentials of assumed identity. YHVH/LORD gazes upward, extending vision beyond present limits, while Elohim prepares to manifest that multiplicity.

Faith Counted as Righteousness

And he had faith in the Lord, and it was put to his account as righteousness

Symbolism: Faith is the full assumption of I AM. Holding the feeling of the wish fulfilled aligns YHVH/LORD with Ehyeh/I AM, and Elohim enforces it, producing manifest reality.

A Deeper Assurance: Abram’s Question

And he said, O Lord God, how may I be certain that it will be mine?

Symbolism: Seeking confirmation reflects the shift from belief to inner knowing. YHVH/LORD questions, Ehyeh/I AM maintains certainty, and Elohim continues enforcement of the assumed state.

The Covenant Ritual: Dividing the Animals

Abram brings a heifer, a goat, and a ram—each three years old—plus a turtledove and a young pigeon. He cuts the larger animals in two (birds remain whole) and lays the pieces opposite each other.

Symbolism: The divided animals represent old states of consciousness sacrificed to make way for the new I AM. Exposing their innards signals conscious acknowledgment and surrender, allowing Elohim to enforce transformation.

The Dark Sleep and Prophetic Vision

As the sun was setting, a deep sleep fell on Abram, and a dark, dreadful fear surrounded him. God said to him: Your seed will live in a land that isn’t theirs. They will serve those people and suffer for 400 years. But I will judge that nation, and your seed will come out of it with great riches. You, Abram, will die peacefully, and be buried at a good old age. Then, in the fourth generation, they will return to this land—because right now, the wickedness of the Amorites hasn’t yet reached its full measure.

Symbolism: The deep sleep represents YHVH/LORD handing the assumed I AM to the inner mechanism. Darkness and fear are natural resistance before change. The “seed” signifies the planted assumption; the 400 years mark prolonged limitation until Elohim manifests the new reality. “Great riches” are the tangible outcome of inner persistence.

The Smoking Firepot and Blazing Torch

When the sun had set and darkness had fallen, a smoking firepot with a blazing torch appeared and passed between the pieces.
  • Smoking Firepot: the unseen, transformative action of Elohim on assumed identity.
  • Blazing Torch: conscious YHVH/LORD awareness of the I AM.
  • Passing between the pieces: the sealing of the triad; the union of present consciousness and assumed identity produces reality.

The Seal of the Covenant: The Promised Land

On that day the Lord made a covenant with Abram, saying, To your offspring I give this land… from the Wadi of Egypt to the great river, the Euphrates.

Symbolism: The land is expanded consciousness. Defining its borders illustrates the scope of imagination; Elohim now enforces the fully assumed I AM within consciousness.

Living the Promise: A New State of Being

Genesis 15, interpreted through Neville Goddard’s framework, presents a nine-step process for conscious manifestation:

  1. Receive the inner assurance (shield and reward).
  2. Notice early resistance (childlessness).
  3. Lift vision to infinite potentials (stars).
  4. Believe without evidence (faith counted as righteousness).
  5. Surrender deeper doubts (assurance).
  6. Release old patterns (divided animals).
  7. Endure unseen work (deep sleep and darkness).
  8. Seal the assumption (smoking firepot and blazing torch).
  9. Claim the limitless outcome (Promised Land).

Walk each step internally, and Elohim will manifest the I AM you assume—just as the Promise was fulfilled for Abram.

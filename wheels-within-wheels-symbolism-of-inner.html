Ezekiel’s Wheels: A Pictorial Image of God

"The image of a wheel turning within a wheel illustrates the courtroom of consciousness: how your outer life moves in alignment with the I AM you assume within.

It embodies the living declaration of 'I AM the Alpha and the Omega' in Revelation 1:8, enforced by the statutes of Elohim."

One profound image in the Bible appears in Ezekiel: “a wheel in the middle of a wheel” (Ezekiel 1:16). At first, it may seem abstract or mechanical. Yet, through the framework of the courtroom of consciousness, we see it as a symbol of inner transformation, conscious movement, and the multidimensional nature of identity.

The Vision: Ezekiel’s Wheels

Ezekiel writes:

“This was the appearance and structure of the wheels: they sparkled like topaz, and all four looked alike. Each appeared to be made like a wheel intersecting a wheel. As they moved, they would go in any one of the four directions the creatures faced; the wheels did not turn about as the creatures went. Their rims were high and awesome, and all four rims were full of eyes all around."

— Ezekiel 1:15-18 NKJV

Seen through the key, these wheels are not mechanical but operational: they depict consciousness at work. The creatures represent YHVH/LORD, the present consciousness engaging each aspect of self. The wheels are Ehyeh/I AM, the assumed identities that YHVH/LORD declares, moving in harmony with the creatures. The eyes and the perfect structure of the wheels signify Elohim, the Judges & Rulers enforcing the statutes of reality and ensuring the assumed identity is actualised.

“And the edges of the four wheels were full of eyes round about.” — Ezekiel 10:12

Elohim: The Plural Majesty of Consciousness

To fully grasp Ezekiel’s wheels, we must consider the Hebrew name for God: Elohim (אֱלֹהִים). This plural noun—“mighty ones” or “divine ones”—expresses the plurality of internal governance. Elohim is the judicial structure within consciousness that enforces the outcome once YHVH/LORD assumes Ehyeh/I AM.

Just as the wheels move seamlessly in every direction, Elohim operates impartially, executing the law of creation so that the inner “I AM” governs outward reality. Plural yet unified, Elohim demonstrates the multidimensional nature of imagination itself.

The Inner and Outer Wheels: Consciousness in Motion

Here the outer wheel corresponds to the physical world and external circumstances—the results we perceive. The inner wheel is the assumed identity, Ehyeh/I AM, that YHVH/LORD inhabits at the core of consciousness. This inner wheel drives all outer motion, showing the Ask → Believe → Receive principle in action:

  • Ask: YHVH/LORD recognises a desire within consciousness.
  • Believe: Ehyeh/I AM assumes the identity of the desired state internally.
  • Receive: Elohim enforces the outcome, manifesting it in reality.

The perpetual rotation of wheels reminds us that consciousness is always in motion. As the inner I AM turns, the outer world aligns accordingly. Change begins within, and the outer conditions follow the assumed identity.

The Many Eyes: Self-Awareness and Watchfulness

The eyes covering the wheels signify awareness. They represent the faculty of observing one’s inner dialogue, emotions, and self-concepts. This awareness is crucial: without noticing where YHVH/LORD is moving and which I AM is assumed, Elohim cannot enforce the desired outcome effectively.

Spirals, Layers, and Dimensions of Consciousness

A wheel within a wheel illustrates the non-linear, layered nature of self-perception. Repeating patterns are not failures but deeper spirals of growth. Each turn is a new enforcement of identity by Elohim, guiding YHVH/LORD toward full self-realisation through the assumed I AM.

From Elohim to Legion: Original and Fragmented States

Elohim represents the unified, original state of awareness—whole, multidimensional, and in alignment. Fragmentation, as symbolised in the story of Legion (Mark 5:1–20), shows consciousness splintered into limiting beliefs and conflicting inner voices. Ezekiel’s vision reminds us that the pure creative power within is always available: a central wheel of imagination waiting to be assumed fully.

Conclusion: You Are the Centre of the Divine Movement

The wheel within a wheel shows that reality is not separate from your inner self. The inner wheel—Ehyeh/I AM assumed by YHVH/LORD—is the creative core. The outer wheel responds, and Elohim enforces the manifestation of your assumed identity.

By consciously assuming your I AM, observing YHVH/LORD’s present moves, and allowing Elohim to enforce, you participate directly in the machinery of creation. Each rotation of the inner wheel is a declaration of identity, moving the outer world in harmony with your consciousness.

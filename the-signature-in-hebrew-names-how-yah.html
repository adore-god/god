Yah, Iah, and El in Names: Identifiers of the I AM

The Bible is a profound symbolic narrative of unfolding self-perception. One of the most revealing keys to this inner drama lies in the names—especially those ending with -yah, -iah, or -el. These endings encode the presence and action of identifying names that point to the creative consciousness Neville Goddard highlights as the source of all manifestation.

This is why the genealogies are so important in the Bible, especially in the lineage of Jesus.

The Meaning Behind “-yah,” “-iah,” and “-el”

-yah and -iah are shortened forms of Yahweh (YHWH), the sacred name of God meaning “I AM”, the self-aware creative presence revealed in Exodus 3:14 as “I AM THAT I AM.” Names with this suffix signify the active presence of this conscious “I AM” working within the character or state symbolised.

-el means “God” or “mighty one,” linked to Elohim, another divine name often understood as the creative power and authority behind all being. Names ending in -el connect the character to divine strength, power, or authority manifesting through imagination.

Examples of Names and Their Symbolic Meanings

  • Isaiah (Yeshayahu): “Yahweh is salvation” — the “I AM” presence as the source of deliverance.

  • Jeremiah (Yirmeyahu): “Yahweh will exalt” — conscious “I AM” raising the state.

  • Hezekiah: “The strength of Yahweh” — the empowering force of “I AM.”

  • Michael: “Who is like God?” — a rhetorical question affirming divine supremacy, with -el emphasising the might of the creative consciousness.

  • Ezekiel: “God strengthens” — the power of Elohim energising transformation and renewal.

Each name reveals psychological modes: the character’s role or state reflects an aspect of self-perception manifesting through imagination. You can read the full list of psychological modes in the lineage of Christ here.

The Ultimate Example: Jesus

The name Jesus (Hebrew Yehoshua or Yeshua) means “Yahweh is salvation” or intimately, “I AM saves.” This is the supreme example of the “I AM” consciousness acting as the source of transformation and deliverance.

In Neville Goddard’s teachings, Jesus is not a distant figure but the living embodiment of the awakened imagination—the self perceived “I AM” within each of us. The name itself declares the truth that your awareness of being is your true salvation and creative power.

The Unified Creative Principle in Names

Whether ending with -yah, -iah, or -el, these names point to the same underlying reality: the creative “I AM” consciousness expressing itself in various forms and functions. This reflects Neville Goddard’s core teaching that the self-aware imagination is the source of all manifestation.

The divine names encoded in these suffixes are linguistic markers of this creative process — the inner presence that assumes, feels, and brings reality into being.

Why This Matters

Recognising this pattern shifts your view from seeing biblical names as just labels to understanding them as symbolic declarations of the conscious “I AM” at work within you. This insight empowers you to consciously engage your imagination, embody your desired states, and master your creative power.
